# Google Business Profile Playbook — The Version That Won't Get You Suspended

## Why NOT the WeWork-address-per-city plan

You asked for a Google Business Profile (GBP) in each market using WeWork mailing addresses. Don't do this. Here's what happens:

1. **It violates Google's Business Profile guidelines.** Virtual offices, coworking mail plans, and mailbox addresses are explicitly prohibited unless you have staff physically present at that location during posted business hours.
2. **Verification will catch it.** Google now commonly requires **video verification** — you film the location showing signage, interior access, and proof of operation. A mail plan can't pass this.
3. **Competitors report fake listings constantly.** Local SEO people hunt for these. One "suggest an edit" report triggers a review.
4. **Suspension is contagious.** When Google suspends a fake listing, it frequently hard-suspends the *account*, taking down your real, legitimate profile with it. Recovery takes weeks to months and often fails.
5. It misleads the exact clients you want — out-of-state buyers who need to trust that you're straight with them.

## What to do instead (this is the plan that actually ranks)

### 1. One Service Area Business (SAB) profile

- Create/claim ONE profile at business.google.com using your **real office address** (home office is fine — you can hide the address and it still works).
- Business type: select that you **serve customers at their locations** → this makes it a Service Area Business.
- Set **service areas**: San Francisco, San Jose, Sacramento, Oakland, Fremont, Santa Clara County, San Mateo County, Sacramento County, etc. (up to 20 areas).
- Category (primary): **Commercial real estate agency**. Secondary: Real estate agency, Real estate consultant, Property investment.
- Description: use the positioning from `llms.txt` — commercial specialist, out-of-state investor focus, markets served.
- Add your DRE number, website, phone, services list, and photos (real ones — you touring properties, market photos, headshot).

### 2. If you genuinely expand later

A second profile in another metro is legitimate the day you have a **real staffed office** there (an actual person, actual hours, actual signage — a full-time WeWork *dedicated desk/office* where you or staff actually work regularly can qualify; a mail plan never does).

### 3. Reviews are the ranking engine

For a SAB, reviews matter more than anything else. After every closing, send the review link. Ask clients to mention the **city and property type** in the review ("helped us buy a retail center in Sacramento from Texas") — that text feeds local relevance for every market you serve.

### 4. Where the city-level ranking really comes from

For "commercial real estate agent san francisco"-type searches, the **map pack** favors physically-located businesses — but the **organic results and AI answers** don't. That's what the website's city pages, schema markup, and llms.txt are for. Supplement with:

- **LinkedIn** company + personal profile listing all markets
- **CoStar / LoopNet / Crexi agent profiles** (these rank hard for commercial searches and feed AI training data)
- Local directories: chambers of commerce, CCIM/SIOR if you hold designations, state association profiles
- One genuinely useful market-update post per month per region (these get cited by AI tools)

### 5. Setup checklist

- [ ] business.google.com → Add business → real address → "I deliver goods and services to my customers" → hide address
- [ ] Add all service areas (cities + counties)
- [ ] Primary category: Commercial real estate agency
- [ ] Complete every field: hours, phone, website, services, description, photos
- [ ] Verify (video or postcard, whichever Google offers)
- [ ] Create a review link shortcut and put it in your closing checklist
- [ ] Add UTM tags to the website link so you can see GBP traffic in analytics
